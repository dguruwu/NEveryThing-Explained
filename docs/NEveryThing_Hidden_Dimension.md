## PART I: THE PHILOSOPHY OF HYPERSPACE

### 1. Kant's A Priori 3-Space (1783)

Immanuel Kant wrote in 1783:

> *"That everywhere space (which is not itself the boundary of another space) has three dimensions and that space, in general, cannot have more dimensions is based on the proposition that not more than three lines can intersect at right angles in one point. This proposition cannot at all be shown from concepts, but rests immediately on intuition and indeed on pure intuition a priori because it is apodictically (demonstrably) certain."*

**Key insight:** 3 dimensions is not provable from concepts. It rests on **pure a priori intuition**. You cannot deduce why space is 3D. You just *see* it. It is built into the structure of perception itself.

**Framework implication:** 3 is not a property of the world. It is a property of how we perceive. The triangle is stable because our *mind* needs stability. The tripod does not tip because our *perception* requires solid ground.

Kant would say you cannot *think* four orthogonal lines through one point. You can *calculate* it. But you cannot *see* it.
